## Technical Supervision

### Value Proposition

In industrial projects, technical deviations are rarely discovered at the moment they occur. They surface at the end, when the cost of correction is significantly higher than it would have been to control them in process. The absence of specialized technical supervision during fabrication, assembly, or construction is one of the most frequent causes of cost overruns, specification failures, and contractual disputes.

This service establishes a continuous technical control mechanism that verifies compliance with engineering, specifications, and applicable standards at every stage of the project, protecting the client's investment and reducing the margin of technical risk.

---

### Service Scope

- Technical review and approval of manufacturer and contractor documentation
- Supervision of shop fabrication processes (welding, machining, assembly, heat treatment)
- Quality control on material and equipment reception
- Verification of assembly procedures and installation sequences
- Monitoring of compliance with technical and engineering specifications
- Issuance of progress reports, non-conformities, and corrective actions
- Participation in acceptance tests, pre-commissioning, and commissioning
- Technical management of scope changes during execution

---

### Key Benefits

- Reduced rework by detecting deviations at the moment they occur, not at the end
- Technical risk mitigation through continuous, documented monitoring throughout execution
- Protection of the client's margin by preventing contractor errors from being absorbed by the project budget
- Verifiable regulatory compliance with supervision records ready for audit or contractual settlement
- Greater control over the final quality of the delivered asset, with technical evidence at each stage

---

### Work Methodology

1. **Engineering package and contract review**: full understanding of scope, applicable specifications, and technical acceptance criteria before supervision begins.
2. **Supervision plan**: definition of inspection points, visit frequency, hold point criteria, and required documentation.
3. **Field or shop supervision**: technical presence at critical process stages with systematic recording of observations.
4. **Report issuance**: periodic progress reports, non-conformity records, and follow-up on their effective closure.
5. **Change control**: technical evaluation of any modification to the original scope and its impact on quality, cost, and schedule.
6. **Technical closure**: verification of final deliverables, participation in acceptance tests, and issuance of the supervision closure report.
